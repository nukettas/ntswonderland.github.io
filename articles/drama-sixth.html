When was the time that you learned you needed to be a fighter?

Written By NT July 17, 2023
Drama

I was at my grandmother's summer house. It was a family gathering, everyone was there, my uncles and aunties, my cousins. Being around six years old, there were noticeable age differences between me and my cousins, yet we were all quite young. The atmosphere took a turn when disagreements arose among the adults over something I can't recall now.

It was clear, even to us youngsters, that something wasn't right. In an attempt to shield us from the escalating conflict, my grandmother decided to take me and my cousins to another location. Despite our tender age, we understood the tension in the air. Silently, we walked alongside my grandmother, our emotions threatening to overwhelm us. Remarkably, my grandmother didn't press us to talk; instead, she shared in our solemn silence. On that particular day, there was a warning of rough seas, but my grandmother chose to take us to the beach regardless. As we approached the shore after about a 20-minute walk, an empty stretch of sand greeted us.

I remember that my grandmother stood in front of us and said, now we are going to learn how not to fall. We held hands together, walked into the sea. We fought with waves, we choked sometimes, we fell but we laughed at the end of it. I remember that my grandmother was also laughing with us, she was like one of us that day, she was like our friend. That was the date that I knew I had to be a fighter if I wanted to laugh.
