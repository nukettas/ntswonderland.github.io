Drowning in the Echoes of Morning

Written By NT June 9, 2023
Confrontation

Today, she woke up in a bad mood. Even though it was the beginning of the day, she felt tired. Her back was sore, and her eyes felt heavy. She sat up but couldn't find the strength to get out of bed, so she rolled back under the covers. Thankfully, it was her day off, with no work, nowhere to go, and nothing to do.

As usual, her thoughts started racing as soon as she woke up, but this time they were particularly distressing. "It's not even 10 a.m., please leave me alone," she pleaded with herself. Unfortunately, it didn't help. Her mind kept replaying terrible memories involving her friends and family. She felt numb and couldn't even move to turn her left side. "Why are you doing this to me?" she wondered, trying to grasp what triggered these feelings today, but she couldn't find a reason.

She knew her mind was always talking to her, nonstop. But at least it could have waited until the end of the day to remind her of those memories, not at 10 freaking a.m.! She wasn't quite asleep, yet she wasn't fully awake either. Suddenly, her phone rang—it was her long-distance boyfriend. She didn't even try to fake a smile as they started talking because she lacked the energy to do so.

When he didn't notice how depressed she was during their conversation and didn't ask why she was still in bed or why she looked so down today (she knew she looked terrible, for sure), she felt strange. After they hung up, she felt even more tension on her shoulders. Nobody, not even the most trustworthy and connected person she had ever known, could tell that something was wrong. She had never felt so exhausted, alone, and unheard. Under the weight of it all, her eyes surrendered to the pressure, and she quickly fell asleep.
