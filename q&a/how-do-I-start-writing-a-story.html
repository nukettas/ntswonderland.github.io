How do I start writing a story?

Written By NT October 18, 2023
Writing-Tips

Hey, I heard you're interested in writing a short story. That's awesome! Starting a story can be a bit daunting, but don't worry; I've got some tips that might help. You know, it's all about finding your inspiration and letting your creativity flow.



First things first, find what inspires you. It could be anything, really. Think about that news article you read last week, or that character you can't get out of your head. Inspiration is the spark that gets the creative engine running.

I can hear that you are asking “Alright, but once I have an idea, what's next?” Good question! After finding your inspiration, it's essential to figure out what you want to say with your story. Is there a particular message, emotion, or theme you'd like to convey? This will give your story a purpose and direction.

So, do you need to start with a character? Exactly! Developing a strong main character can be a game-changer. Think about their background, what they desire, and what's holding them back. The more you know about your character, the more relatable and engaging they become.

What about the setting NT? It's like the backdrop of your story. Decide where and when your story takes place. A well-chosen setting can add depth and atmosphere. Now, you'll want to create a basic outline for your story. Nothing too fancy, just a starting point. What's the opening scene that will draw readers in, the complications that arise, and how everything will wrap up.


Now, you've got all that, but how do you start actually writing? Now comes the fun part. Start writing. Seriously, don't worry too much about perfection at this stage. You're building the foundation. Just get your ideas down on paper. As you write, focus on your story's opening. It should grab your reader's attention and introduce your main character and the central conflict. You can start with something intriguing, like a captivating scene, a provocative statement, or even a question that piques curiosity.


To summarize the thought process and a step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Find Inspiration Begin by seeking inspiration. This can come from various sources: a personal experience, a news article, an interesting character you've thought of, a vivid dream, a piece of artwork, or a unique setting.

Step 2: Define Your Theme or Message Think about what you want your story to convey. Is there a particular theme, message, or emotion you want to explore or communicate to your readers? This will help you focus your story.

Step 3: Develop a Main Character Create a protagonist. Consider their background, desires, fears, and the internal conflicts they might face. A well-defined character can often be the cornerstone of a compelling story.

Step 4: Establish a Setting Decide where and when your story takes place. The setting can influence the tone and atmosphere of your story, so choose it thoughtfully.

Step 5: Create a Plot Outline Outline the basic structure of your story. This can be as simple as a beginning, middle, and end. What event or conflict sets the story in motion, what complications arise, and how does it all resolve?

Step 6: Start Writing With your inspiration, theme, character, setting, and plot in mind, begin writing. Don't worry too much about making it perfect at this stage. The goal is to get words on the page. You can refine and edit later.

Step 7: Focus on the Opening The opening of your story is crucial. It should grab the reader's attention and introduce the main character and the central conflict. You can start with an intriguing scene, a provocative statement, or a question.

Step 8: Use Descriptive Language As you write, use vivid and descriptive language to paint a picture for your readers. Engage their senses and emotions to make the story more immersive.

Step 9: Maintain Tension and Conflict Keep the reader engaged by introducing or building on conflicts, obstacles, or challenges for your protagonist. This will create a sense of anticipation and curiosity.

Step 10: Keep Writing and Revise Don't be overly critical of your first draft. The most important thing is to keep writing. You can always go back and revise, add depth to characters, and refine the plot and prose later on.

Step 11: Seek Feedback Once you have a draft you're satisfied with, consider seeking feedback from trusted readers or writing groups. Their insights can help you refine your story further.



Just remember, writing is a process, and it's okay if your first draft isn't perfect. You can always revise and add more details later. And, when you're ready, don't hesitate to share your work with others for feedback. Happy writing!
