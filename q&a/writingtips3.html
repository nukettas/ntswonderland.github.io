Essential Elements of a Short Story: What 3 Things Should a Short Story Have?

Written By NT September 28, 2023
Writing-Tips

A well-crafted short story should have three essential elements that make it both engaging and memorable:


Characters: Characters are the heart and soul of any short story. They are the individuals who populate the world of your short story. They bring the narrative to life, allowing readers to connect with their struggles, desires, and journeys. Whether it's a complex protagonist, a compelling antagonist, or a cast of intriguing supporting characters. Well-developed characters have distinct personalities, desires, and flaws, making them relatable and engaging for readers.
Example: Character Name: Emily Description: A 32-year-old librarian with a passion for ancient history. Motivation: To uncover the secrets of an enigmatic artifact she found in a dusty old book. Flaw: She is overly cautious and tends to overthink, which leads to missed opportunities.
In this example, Emily is a well-developed character with a clear personality, motivation, and a flaw that can create tension or conflict in the story.


Conflict: Every short story needs conflict to drive the plot forward and create tension. It can be an internal struggle within a character (man vs. self) or an external challenge they must face (man vs. nature, man vs. society, man vs. man). Conflict keeps readers invested in the story, wondering how the characters will resolve their issues.
Example: Conflict: Emily must decide whether to risk her job and reputation to pursue the artifact's secrets, which could hold the key to a hidden civilization's history.
In this example, the conflict is both internal (Emily's inner struggle to make a difficult decision) and external (the potential consequences of her actions).


Resolution:A satisfying resolution is essential to provide closure to the story. It's the moment when loose ends are tied up, questions are answered, and readers gain a sense of fulfillment. The resolution doesn't always have to be a happy ending; it should be appropriate for the story's theme and genre.
Example: Resolution: Emily decides to risk everything and pursue the artifact's secrets. She uncovers a long-lost civilization's history, which not only enhances her career but also brings her a sense of personal fulfillment.
In this example, the resolution brings a satisfying conclusion to Emily's internal and external conflicts, leading to personal growth and success.


By effectively incorporating these elements into your short story, you create a narrative with relatable characters, engaging conflicts, and a resolution that resonates with your readers, making for a compelling and memorable literary experience.


Incorporating these three elements—characters, conflict, and resolution—into your short stories ensures they have the depth and structure needed to captivate readers and leave a lasting impact.
